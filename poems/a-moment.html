A Moment

a poem for clarity, courage, and confession

I need a moment to breathe

For life to stop and for all to freeze

I need a moment to think

Of how I could make all my problems shrink

I need a moment to gather my emotions

To do that without causing a commotion

I need a moment to feel

To let these feelings take the wheel

I need a moment to remember

How love once gave me color

I need a moment to reminisce

About touches, looks, and stolen bliss.

I need a moment to unhide,

The spark that dances when you’re by my side.

One day you’re fire, the next just flame,

And I’m left wondering if you feel the same.

I need a moment to decode your eyes,

To know if this is truth or pretty lies.

Kisses like stories we never wrote,

But silence the next day catches in my throat.

I need a moment to ask aloud,

If we’re something real or just allowed

To blur the lines and share the space,

But never claim a steady place.

I need a moment to confess,

That when you call, my heart says “yes.”

That your voice can calm my storm inside,

And yet some days, you run and hide.

I need a moment to be brave,

To break the silence, ride the wave.

To say what lingers in the air

I care for you. Do you care?
